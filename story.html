Cultural Spotlight: Camping in the United States

Camping is a popular outdoor activity in many parts of the United States. Families and friends visit forests, lakes, and parks to sleep in tents, cook over campfires, and explore nature together. Camping teaches people how to care for the outdoors and stay safe while enjoying nature.

This fun outdoor adventure story for children follows three best friends as they leave the city for their very first camping trip. With planning, teamwork, and curiosity, the City Squad learns new skills, explores nature, and discovers that trying new things can be exciting.

Big Ideas in this story

  • Planning ahead helps adventures go smoothly
  • Working together makes difficult tasks easier
  • Taking care of the environment helps protect nature for others

Core early learning skills

  • Social-emotional learning (SEL): Managing worries and building confidence
  • STEM: Learning about nature, animals, and outdoor safety
  • Sequencing: Following steps to set up tents, build fires, and prepare food

Talk & explore together

Try these conversation starters

  • Would you like to go camping? What would you bring?
  • Why do you think is it important to stay on trails and leave nature as you found it?

Activities

  • Pretend to Camp: Use blankets, pillows, or chairs to pretend to set up a tent. Work together to decide where to place your “camp.”
  • Nature Sounds Listening: Listen for sounds outside, like birds, wind, or insects. Talk about what you hear and how nature sounds are different from city sounds.
  • Plan Your Own Adventure: Draw a picture of where you would go camping. Would it be near a lake, forest, or mountains?
